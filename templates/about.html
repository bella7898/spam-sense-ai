Our Story

Spam Sense AI was collaboratively developed by students over the course of three-weeks at AI camp under the guidance of camp mentor Cason Shepard, with the aim of providing accessible and dependable tools for identifying spam and scam emails with high quality and efficiency for users worldwide.

Read our story below

Brainstorming our Idea

Intially, we focused on identifying a number of potential projects that would align with Natural Language Processing (NLP). Eventually, after conducting research and checking the viability given our circumstances in the camp, we decided upon a project which would identify user spam emails & SMS and named it "Spam-Sense".

Collecting our Data

After finalizing our project idea, the next pivotal step we took was centered around data collection. We explored a number of platforms such as Kaggle, Huggingface, and several others, eventually securing two distinct datasets. The first dataset was for email spam, while the second dataset catered to SMS spam..


Preprocessing the Data

Following the data collection, we needed to pre-process it using a number of NLP techniques including word tokenization, removing punctuation & special characters, removing empty cells, etc. We also combined and labeled our datasets for the purpose of convenience. This step would be crucial for later data analysis and would help improve the accuracy of our model.

Training and Testing

We later trained and tested our model (DistilBERT) which utilized the 80/20 split method. Initially, we were only able to achieve around 69% accuracy, however we were eventually able to reach around 98.8% accuracy after adjusting the learning rate, weight decay, and the number of epochs.

Developing our Website

The tech stack we used for web development included HTML/CSS and Bootstrap for the frontend and Flask for the backend - connecting our model to the website, which can be found on the 'Demo' page.
